How Does Solar Work For Your Home

Essentially what happens is that solar panels record the sun’s energy and then work to convert that energy into power for your home. After sunlight hits solar panels, it’s captured within photovoltaic cells and transported to an inverter where it’s converted from direct current DC electricity to usable AC electricity. From there, the electricity is ready to be used by you and your home or is sent out to the grid to be used by your neighbors. In some other cases, the power might be stored in battery banks

Excess solar power above storage capacity is exported to the grid. Your power provider will compensate you for the energy your home system produces.

Light photons from the sun are absorbed by the solar panels on your roof, which generates a direct current (DC).

The AC current goes through your main switchboard, enabling the supply of electrical power to your entire home.

Solar batteries can be used to store excess solar power for later use.

An inverter system converts the DC current into an alternating current (AC), which can now be used to power your appliances.
